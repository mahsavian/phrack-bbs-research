# From BBS to Blackboard: The Pedagogy of the Exploit

This repository contains the primary research artifacts from my Master's thesis (University of Padua). This project investigates the historical evolution of digital expertise within the pre-WWW underground.

## 📄 Research Artifacts
* **[Full Thesis (PDF)](./Thesis_Vian_Phrack_BBS.pdf):** A 100+ page socio-technical autopsy of *Phrack Magazine* and the co-production of hacker expertise.
* **[Research Presentation (PPT)](./Presentation_Vian_Hacker_Pedagogy.pptx):** A visual summary of the methodology (Digital Discourse Analysis) and key findings regarding "informal epistemic cultures."

## 🔍 Abstract
By analyzing the archives of *Phrack Magazine* (1985–2016), this research maps how underground digital communities functioned as alternative university systems. It explores the "Mobility of Knowledge"—how technical "exploits" were transformed into pedagogical tools, eventually influencing formal cybersecurity education and institutional governance.

## 🛠️ Methodology
* **Digital Discourse Analysis (DDA)**
* **Historical Archival Research**
* **Socio-Technical Mapping**
